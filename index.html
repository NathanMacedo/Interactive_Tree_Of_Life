The Alchemical Great Work

🜂 Fire
Meaning: Fire is associated with spirit, transformation, and energy — the active, masculine principle. It represents the intense heat that purifies.
Symbolic Use: Fire breaks down material substances (calcination) and purifies them, symbolizing the soul’s journey through trials to enlightenment.
🜄 Water
Meaning: Water is the passive, feminine principle — emotion, intuition, and the unconscious. It represents fluidity, fertility, and nourishment.
Symbolic Use: In alchemy, Water represents dissolution: breaking down rigid structures to allow adaptation and transformation.
🜁 Air
Meaning: Air stands for intellect, communication, and the breath of life — mental activity, thought, rationality, movement, and freedom.
Symbolic Use: Air symbolizes transformation through intellectual insight, relating to evaporation, where volatile elements are extracted and purified.
🜃 Earth
Meaning: Earth represents matter, stability, and the physical body — the foundation for the work of transformation.
Symbolic Use: Earth is the starting point: the base material that will be purified into something of higher value.
🜍 Sulfur — The Soul
Meaning: Sulfur symbolizes the soul, the active principle, the spark of life — passion, fire, vitality, and will.
Symbolic Use: Sulfur is the agent of combustion or spiritual fire, fueling the work of transformation and purification.
🜔 Salt — The Body
Meaning: Salt symbolizes the body and the crystallization of the physical world — the stabilizing principle of preservation.
Symbolic Use: Salt is associated with coagulation. It is the final product of alchemical work, representing the reunion of spirit and matter.
☿ Mercury — The Spirit
Meaning: Mercury represents the spirit, the volatile principle, and the connection between physical and spiritual realms. Fluid, changeable, adaptive.
Symbolic Use: Mercury is central to transmutation: it dissolves and transforms substances, carrying the potential for the Philosopher's Stone.
Tria Prima — The Three Primes
Sulfur, Mercury, and Salt together are the three essential principles of creation and transformation:
  • 🜍 Sulfur — the soul, the active principle
  • ☿ Mercury — the spirit, the volatile principle
  • 🜔 Salt — the body, the stabilizing principle
